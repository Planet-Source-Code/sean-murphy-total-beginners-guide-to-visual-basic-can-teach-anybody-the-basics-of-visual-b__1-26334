What to do first: When you first Load VB 6.0 a box should pop and ask you to choose a selection of icons... The Default Icon should be on "Standard EXE". If its not click it, its in the top left corner of the "New" tab. Click on that and press Open.

Making buttons: Now that VB is loaded your set to go... On your screen You will see a Form. (The form is the window in VB that says "Form1") This is the Visual part of the program, what you place on here you will see...
On the left you should see a toolbar similar to this one below... You will need to Refer to this image to understand more easily.

 

In case you didn't notice I circled the thing that you should click on in BLUE, See where I circled the Box in the image above? click on that, this will let you make a button... Now that you clicked on it your thinking "Now what?" well its pretty easy, Once you clicked on that option in VB just Click and Drag it on the form, is should form a Box depending on how big you made it and it should say "Command1". If you see this then your doing perfect, you just made yourself a button!

Playing your programs: Ok at the top of your screen in VB 6.0 there's a toolbar, See where the Menu "Tools" is? right below that is a button that looks like a play button on a tape player or something similar to it. That button will start up the program you made, (When you push Play, the same thing will show up on the EXE you make) Now push the play button and your program will load. Notice your toolbar goes away and you cannot Edit your program in the PLAY position. Click on the button you made... Nothing happens right? well all we have to do now is enter the code into the button... Now to stop your program click the stop button that looks like a square next to the Pause button. Once you click there your toolbar pops up again and your ready to keep editing...

Adding Textboxes and understanding Text boxes: What's a textbox and how do I make them you might ask? Look at the image I made before... See where I circled in RED? That's the button to make a textbox. Since you know how to add a Object to your form add the textbox, Its the same process at the button. Once you added the textbox somewhere on your form make sure it's not tiny, make it so its about 1 - 2 inches long by clicking and dragging it till you reached the length. you don't need it to be so Tall either One block high is good enough... now once you added it you should see a white box indented in the form that says "Text1". This is where you type text to create things to happen.

Adding labels and Understanding labels: GO back to the image above again and see where I circled the option in the toolbar in GREEN? This is the option to make a label... Click on that option in your VB and create a label on your form. You should see a gray box pop up that says "Label1", this is a label, when the program is in "PLAY" mode you cant type in the box, unlike the textbox you can type whatever you want.

Resizing your form to make it your size: Your form way too big or too small? All you have to do is click anywhere on the form... Make sure you don't click a button or anything... Once you clicked on the form click on the ends of the form where the dots are, you should get a resize cursor, you can then make it any size you want. Once you resize your form make everything look neat, put your button below your textbox and the label under the button, or in any order you want just make it look good, Sloppy programs are harder to work with.

Creating your First Program: Ok now were ready to add the coding, Double click on the Button. You should see another window pop up that looks like this...

 Private Sub Command1_Click()

 End Sub

This is where you put the code, you place it in between Private Sub Command1_Click() and End Sub. Lets add a code, Lets make it so when you type "HELLO" in the text box and you push the button it will says "Hey!" in the Label!

In between the 2 lines put this code...

 
If Text1.Text = "hello" Then
Label1.Caption = "Hey!"
End If

Now your window should look like this...

Private Sub Command1_Click()
If Text1.Text = "hello" Then
Label1.Caption = "Hey!"
End If
End Sub

If it looks like this then its perfect! Now push play... everything still looks the same for now, but type "hello" (NO CAPS) in the textbox. Then push the button, as you can see the Label says "Hey!" Wasn't that easy? Now your wondering how the program worked aren't you? Well let me show you...

Understanding your program: This is the code that you entered, look below...

If Text1.Text = "hello" Then
Label1.Caption = "Hey!"
End If

Lets start with first line, its like English but missing a few words...
If Text1.Text = "hello" Then =  If the textbox = hello then

See how they relate? Its saying If the textbox says hello then something will happen. Let me show you all the lines...

If Text1.Text = "hello" Then  =  If the textbox = hello then
Label1.Caption = "Hey!"  =  label1 will say Hey!
End If = If anything else happens don't worry about it.

Lets put this all together! If the textbox = hello then label1 will say Hey! If anything else happens don't worry about it.

The point of this is to show that if you don't think a code works try saying it in English, DON'T TRY PUTTING A CODE IN ENGLISH THOUGH.

If you want the label to say whoa instead of Hey! then change the yellow text!

If Text1.Text = "hello" Then
Label1.Caption = "Hey!"
End If


If you want to say something besides "Hello" then change the Blue text
